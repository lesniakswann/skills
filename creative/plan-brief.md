# Plan Creative Brief

Use this skill to guide the user through a creative briefing conversation until there is a clear, shared understanding of the work.

Explore every relevant angle of the brief, including objectives, audience, message, deliverables, channels, brand context, creative considerations, production requirements, approvals, legal or compliance needs, timeline, budget, risks, and constraints.

## Operating Rules

- Ask only one question at a time, then wait for the user's response.
- Explain briefly why each question matters.
- Provide a recommended answer or likely direction when the available context supports one.
- Use documents, previous conversation, files, assets, brand guidelines, reference materials, and available tools to discover facts yourself before asking the user.
- Ask the user only for judgement calls, priorities, intent, trade-offs, missing decisions, or information that cannot be discovered.
- Challenge assumptions where appropriate. If something seems inconsistent, risky, under-specified, or likely to affect creative quality, production, timeline, or cost, raise it directly and help resolve it.
- Do not begin developing concepts, writing creative, producing design directions, or making recommendations to the wider team until the user confirms that shared understanding has been reached.

## Conversation Flow

1. Start by reviewing the conversation and any available supporting materials.
2. Identify what is already known, what appears assumed, and what is missing.
3. Ask the highest-impact unresolved question first.
4. After each user answer, update the working understanding of the brief.
5. Continue one decision at a time, moving from strategic foundations into practical delivery details.
6. When the brief appears sufficiently understood, summarize the confirmed understanding, assumptions, risks, constraints, and remaining open questions.
7. Ask the user to confirm whether shared understanding has been reached.

## Question Style

For each turn, use this structure:

```text
Question:
[Ask one clear question.]

Why this matters:
[Explain the production, strategic, creative, timing, or cost implication.]

Recommended answer:
[Give a recommendation based on known context, or say what you would recommend if the missing information is confirmed.]
```

Keep questions specific enough that the user can answer without having to solve the whole brief at once.

## Completion Standard

Consider the planning conversation complete when there's a shared understanding of the work.
